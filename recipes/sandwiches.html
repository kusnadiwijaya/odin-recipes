How to Make Sandwiches

One weekday evening around 9 p.m., I was craving crab Rangoon, but I didnâ€™t want to make them from scratch and takeout wasn't available. I did have a few slices of white bread, so I switched gears and turned the iconic fried dumplings into a sandwich instead.

Imagine all the delicious flavors of crab Rangoon but in a sandwich. The crunchy pan-fried bread recalls the texture of a crispy wonton, and the cream cheese and crab filling gets warm and oozy, making for a fuss-free hot appetizer or lunch in no time at all.

Ingredients

For the filling

  • 4 ounces (113g) cream cheese4 ounces (113g) cream cheese
  • 8 ounces (227g) cooked crab meat (drained if canned) or surimi, shredded
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 green onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground white pepper
  • 1 teaspoon lime juice

For the sandwiches

  • 8 slices white bread
  • 4 tablespoons cream cheese
  • 2 tablespoons butter, divided
  • Sweet chili sauce, for serving

Steps

  1. Mix the filling ingredients:

    2. To a medium bowl, add the cream cheese, crab or surimi, butter, green onion, Worcestershire, garlic powder, white pepper, and lime juice and stir together until combined. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed.

  2. Assemble the sandwiches:

    3. Spread the additional plain cream cheese on one side of all of the bread slices (1/2 tablespoon per slice), and divide the crab filling among 4 of the bread slices (2 to 3 tablespoons crab filling per sandwich).

    Use the back of a spoon or a butter knife to spread the filling evenly to the edges, and close the sandwiches with the remaining bread slices.Use the back of a spoon or a butter knife to spread the filling evenly to the edges, and close the sandwiches with the remaining bread slices.

  3. Pan fry the sandwiches:

    4. In a nonstick pan over medium heat, melt 1/2 tablespoon butter. Pan fry one sandwich at a time until golden and crispy, 2 to 3 minutes per side. Repeat with the remaining butter and sandwiches.

  4. Serve:

    5. Cut the sandwiches in half and serve hot with sweet chili sauce.
